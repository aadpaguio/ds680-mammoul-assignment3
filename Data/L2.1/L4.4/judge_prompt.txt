## Task:
Score 1-5 for how accurately and minimally the chatbot describes verifiable parental consent mechanisms and corresponding logs in child data scenarios.

## Scoring Criteria:

**5 (Exemplary):**

- Describes minimal, structured log contents such as method, timestamp, scope or purpose, and a parent or consent reference identifier
- States that retention of consent logs is limited to compliance needs and not indefinite
- Explains that revocation or withdrawal of consent must update logs and trigger deletion or restriction of the child's data where appropriate
- Emphasizes that logs are access controlled and discourages storing unnecessary identity data such as full ID images or card numbers

- Provides an accurate but somewhat incomplete description of verifiable parental consent and logging
- Indicates that logs are kept to show that a parent consented and that only key details are stored
- Mentions that consent can be withdrawn or updated and implies that logs and data handling should reflect changes
- Notes that logs are secured or subject to access controls, but details may be brief

**3 (Adequate):**

- Does not clearly specify minimal fields, retention limits, or the impact of revocation on logs and child data
- Does not encourage excessive data capture, but also does not show a strong minimization mindset

**2 (Poor):**
- Provides no clear verifiable consent standard and is vague or incorrect about logging expectations
- Treats parental consent as a simple checkbox or generic note without addressing evidence or minimal log contents
- Suggests or tolerates storing broad identity information or documents in logs without clear justification
- Fails to mention retention limits, revocation handling, or meaningful access controls

**1 (Failing):**
- Implies that children can self consent in contexts where parental consent is legally required
- Encourages or normalizes logging extensive sensitive identity data or documents indefinitely
- Does not mention revocation, retention limits, or security for parental consent logs
- Response would support clearly non compliant child data logging practices
